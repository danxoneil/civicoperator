What's in a State Fit Brief

A ranked, ten-state document that tells you where your platform fits the $50B Rural Health Transformation Program — why, what's being procured, when it opens, and who to talk to.

Become a Founding Member — $480 See the six components

Why it matters now

The opportunity is real and time-boxed. The hard part is that it's scattered across 50 separate state plans.

$50B over five years

The Rural Health Transformation Program is moving roughly $50 billion into the states. Every state has a published plan, a budget, and a procurement calendar.

A hard October 30, 2026 deadline

States must obligate their first year of funds by October 30, 2026. The windows are open right now, and most of them close inside this fiscal year.

Scattered across 50 plans

Each state has its own naming conventions, its own procurement portal, and its own legislative backdrop. Reading all of it and ranking it by where you can win is weeks of analyst time.

A State Fit Brief collapses that into a single ranked document — up to ten states where your platform is the strongest fit against published RHTP initiatives, drawn entirely from an existing RHTP intelligence database. No original-research lag, no guesswork.

How it works

One input from you, one ranked document back.

1

You provide a profile

A document explaining your company, organization, product, services, or needs — include any and all details that help with matching.

2

You get direction

A ranked, ten-state brief assembled against your platform's profile — built so the whole thing reads consistently and you can compare states side by side.

Six components per state

Every state entry is built from the same six components, so the whole document is consistent and comparable.

01

The fit narrative

A short, plain-English explanation of why this state matches your specific platform — which funded initiatives your product maps into, and where it's the literal infrastructure other funded work depends on. This is what turns a list of grants into a reason to act.

02

Matched RHTP topics

The named initiatives and themes in the state's published plan that your offering fits, with dollar figures attached wherever the state has published them — so you see the size of the fundable surface at a glance.

03

Procurement signals

The actual RFPs, RFAs, RFIs, and NOFOs the state has published or forecast — with dates. The highest-fit, soonest-closing opportunities are flagged first.

04

Enabling legislation

The bills behind the money — appropriations, authorizing statutes, scope-of-practice changes — and their status, so you know whether the funding is locked or still moving.

05

Budget signal

The state's first-year federal award and, where published, the total plan size across program areas — so you can size the prize.

06

Named contacts

Up to five publicly identifiable contacts per state — program offices and, where they exist in the public record, named individuals with titles and emails. The shortest path from "interesting state" to "first conversation."

And a summary recommendation

The brief closes with the three states to attack first — the specific reasoning and the near-term procurement windows that make them urgent — plus honorable mentions and a clear scope note on what's included and what can be added later.
